The Quis est homo? Hierarchies of values ​​and forms of identity from antiquity to the present day

The Student Club of Inter-Epochal Historical Research invites you to the National Interdisciplinary Scientific Session "Quis est homo? Hierarchies of values ​​and forms of identity from antiquity to the present day ”devoted to the changes and persistence of values ​​and identity in the past. It will be held on March 23-25, 2022 in the Column Hall of the Faculty of History at the University of Warsaw.

"Always and everywhere fear," said Lucien Febvre, characterizing the lives of the people of 16th-century France. Functioning in a permanent threat, both from nature and others, had to create completely different hierarchies of values ​​and forms of identity than those we observe in today's world. Hence the first of our questions: can we understand the values ​​and identity of our ancestors at least in the face of such a great change in living conditions?

Surely not everything has changed so radically. Today's scientists are able to easily understand the values ​​that guided Robert Boyle - the Englishman who in the 17th century laid the foundations for the experimental method, without which the existence of today's scientific community would not be possible to imagine. To what extent can we speak of the persistence of the hierarchy of values ​​and forms of identity over the centuries?

Yet it is not only the opposition of change and duration. Different forms of identity and different value hierarchies clash with each other constantly at one and the same time. Let us take a classic question from the 19th century: how to awaken national consciousness among the peasants? Here the peasant and local identity is placed in opposition to the national identity, which leads us to the next question: who and how decides about the hierarchies of values ​​professed by society and the forms of identity adopted?

This year's SKMBH conference will be devoted to all of these and many other issues. We cordially invite you to send abstracts!
